## What ACTUALLY Matters for Cybersecurity (Military Transition)

### High-Priority Certs (Directly Relevant)

**These are what employers look for:**

1. **Security+ (CRITICAL FOR MILITARY‚ÜíCYBER)**
   - DoD 8570 requirement (many government contracts)
   - $400, 6 months study
   - Mandatory for many military jobs transitioning to defense contracting
   - **Market signal:** If you don't have it, many jobs won't hire you

2. **AWS Security Specialty (HIGH for cloud-forward roles)**
   - $150, 2-3 months study
   - Increasingly mandatory for cloud security roles
   - Employers paying 5-15% premium for AWS-certified engineers
   - **Why:** Cloud is now 40% of enterprise security

3. **CISSP (HIGH for senior/architect roles)**
   - $749, 6+ months study
   - Requires 5 years experience to sit for exam
   - Premium for senior roles (managers, architects, consultants)
   - **Market signal:** Signals you're "serious" about security management

4. **Kubernetes Certification (CKA) (MEDIUM-HIGH for infrastructure)**
   - $395, 3-4 months study
   - Kubernetes is THE container orchestration platform
   - If you're doing modern infrastructure, this matters
   - **Why:** Kubernetes skills command 20-30% premiums

### Medium-Priority Certs

**These are nice to have, show depth:**

- **CISM** (compliance/governance focus‚Äînot for technical roles)
- **CEH** (ethical hacking‚Äîvery specific, niche demand)
- **RHCE** (Linux mastery‚Äîvaluable for infrastructure folks)

### Low-Priority Certs

**These are entry-level or outdated:**

- **A+, Network+** (entry-level, doesn't command premium once employed)
- **Splunk Certified** (too specialized, limited demand)
- **Vendor-specific** (often free, don't command premium)
