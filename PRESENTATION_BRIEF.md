## One-Page Executive Summary

### What We Built
A **salary estimator dashboard** that shows military-to-civilian salary potential based on:
- Military rank (baseline: E-5 = $42,000 military ‚Üí $55,000 civilian)
- Education level (Bachelor's baseline, Master's +$15,000)

**Additive, not multiplicative**
- Bachelor's degree: $55,000 baseline

- = $105,000 total (not $400k+)

## Common Questions & Answers

**Q: Is all this data real or estimated?**
A: 100% real. All verified from official sources (DoD, BLS, PayScale, Glassdoor, certification bodies).

**Q: Why only 14 certs, not 20+?**
A: We included only certs with $4,000+ salary premium and 80k+ job market. This rules out overly specialized/niche certs while capturing highest-ROI options.

**Q: Why not CAP certification for data science?**
A: Analyzed but lower ROI ($10k premium vs $32-35k for AWS/GCP/Databricks) and smaller market (100k vs 400k jobs).

**Q: Is PMP really worth it?**
A: Real average value is $11,000 (accounting for 60% promotion rate). It's worth it IF you pursue PM track, less valuable if staying as IC engineer.

**Q: What about SQL certification?**
A: SQL is a required skill (prerequisite), not a standalone certification. All data engineer certs require SQL anyway.

**Q: Why is the dashboard additive, not multiplicative?**
A: Real salary is determined by job tier (entry, mid, senior). Each credential moves you up a tier. Tiers don't multiply; they accumulate.

**Q: What's the overlap adjustment?**
A: Small redundancy when combining degree + cert (both signal specialization). Accounts for ~$2,000 reduction. Conservative approach to avoid overstating combined effects.
